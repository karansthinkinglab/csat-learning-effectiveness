The finding

A healthy 7.8 average was hiding three markets in decline.

Global learner satisfaction rose again this year — the headline looked fine. But the average concealed a widening spread: the strongest market kept climbing while the weakest stalled below target. A Kirkpatrick-framed diagnostic traced the gap to specific programmes, not geography.

The average told a reassuring story

Kirkpatrick L1Reaction data. Global CSAT climbed steadily across eight quarters. Taken alone, the trend line invites a "no action needed" read — which is exactly the trap this diagnostic was built to catch.

02

The weakness was in programmes, not places

Kirkpatrick L2Learning data. Breaking scores down by programme type showed the decline wasn't spread evenly. Onboarding and soft-skills content scored well everywhere; compliance and technical-skills training dragged — and the at-risk markets over-index on exactly those.

04

What the data recommends

L4 · ResultsTied to business outcome. Four moves, sequenced by the size of the gap they close and the effort they take.

1
Localise compliance content for Mexico and the wider LATAM region — the single biggest reaction-score gap.
2
Refresh tech-skills modules against current tooling, starting with Brazil where relevance scores are lowest.
3
Re-pace onboarding in Japan and monitor completion — the leading indicator of its CSAT recovery.
4
Report spread, not just average — add a top-vs-bottom gap metric so future declines surface early.
